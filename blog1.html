5 Reasons to Start Reformer Pilates Today

Whether you're a complete beginner or coming back from injury, reformer pilates offers something for every body and every goal.

If you've been curious about reformer pilates but haven't quite taken the leap, let this be your sign. Over the past few years, reformer pilates has grown from a niche practice to one of the most sought-after workouts in the fitness world — and for very good reason.

1. It's Low Impact, but High Reward

One of the most common misconceptions about pilates is that because it's "gentle," it must be easy. Anyone who's taken a reformer class knows otherwise. The spring-based resistance of the reformer machine challenges your muscles in ways that traditional gym workouts can't replicate — and all without the joint impact of running or heavy lifting. This makes it ideal for people of all fitness levels, including those recovering from injury.

2. It Builds Real, Functional Strength

Reformer pilates doesn't just build visible muscle — it builds the deep stabilising muscles that support your spine, improve your balance, and help you move better in everyday life. Many of our members report that their performance in other sports (running, swimming, golf) improves significantly after consistent pilates training.

"After three months of reformer pilates, I ran my fastest marathon. I hadn't changed my running training at all." — Kevin B., Better Studio Member

3. It Corrects Posture and Reduces Back Pain

If you spend hours at a desk, chances are your posture has suffered. The reformer uniquely targets the postural muscles along the spine, shoulders, and hips. Most members report a noticeable improvement in posture and a reduction in neck and back pain within just 8–10 sessions.

4. It's a Form of Active Mindfulness

Pilates requires your full attention. With each movement, you're focusing on breath, alignment, and muscle activation — which means your mind can't wander the way it might on a treadmill. Many members describe reformer class as the only hour of their week where they're truly present.

5. It Works for Every Body and Every Stage of Life

Whether you're 25 or 65, post-partum or post-surgery, training for a marathon or just starting out — reformer pilates can be adapted to you. Our instructors are trained to modify every exercise, which means the class is as challenging or as gentle as you need it to be.
